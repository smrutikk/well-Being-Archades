Breathing Exercises

Breathing Exercise for Depression

1. Place one hand on your chest and the other on your belly, just below your ribcage.

2. Inhale slowly an deeply throught your nose, allowing your belly to expand. Focus on filling your lungs with air rather than raising your chest.

3. Hold it for a while.

4. Exhale slowly and deeply throught your nose, feeling your belly fall.

5. Continue this slow and rhythemic breathing for several breath cycle(minimum 5 times).

Breathing Exercise for Anxiety

1. Find a quite place to sit comfortably. Fold your legs, rest your hands on knee and close your eyes.

2. Inhale slowly throught your nose for a count of four.

3. Hold your breath for a count of seven.

4. Exhale completely and audibly through your mouth to a count of eight.

5. This completes one breath cycle. Now inhale again and repeat the cycle three more times for a total of four breaths.

Breathing Exercise for Anger Issues

1. Find a quite place to sit comfortably. Fold your legs, rest your hands on knee and close your eyes.

2.Inhale slowly throught your nose for a count of four.

3.Hold your breath for a count of four.

4. Exhale slowly and completely through your mouth for a count of four.

5. Pause and hold your breath for another count of four. Repeat the cycle several times, gradually increasing the duration if comfortable.

Breathing Exercise for Insomia

1.Lie down on your back and close your eyes.

2.Begin by taking a slow, deep and long breath in through your nose.

3. Hold your breath briefly at the top of the inhalation, allowing yourself to fully experience the sensation of a deep breath.

4. Exhale slowly and completely through your nose and repeat the cycle.
